Tracey Ria Bey and John Kenneth Johnson were married Nov. 11 at Bethany Baptist Church in Newark. The Rev. Timothy L. Jones, a pastor at the church, performed the ceremony with the Rev. Charles L. Fischer III, the rector of St. Paul’s Episcopal Church in Atlanta, taking part in the sermon and exchange of vows.
Mrs. Johnson, 40, is a product designer in New York for the Macy’s Merchandising Group, where she specializes in home goods. She graduated from Sarah Lawrence College and received a master’s degree in fine arts and printmaking from the Rhode Island School of Design in Providence.
She is the daughter of Carla N. Bey of Glen Ridge, N.J., and Claude S. Bey of Montclair, N.J. The bride’s father, who is retired, was a principal at University High School in Newark. Her mother, who is also retired, was the district testing coordinator for the high school proficiency assessment test in Newark.
Mr. Johnson, 36, works in New York as a global legal director of compliance for Anheuser-Busch InBev, a beer distributor based in Leuven, Belgium. He graduated from Yale and received a law degree from Columbia.
He is the son of Carmen R. Johnson and Kenneth S. Johnson of Woodmere, N.Y. The groom’s mother retired as a nurse manager in the neurology department of NewYork-Presbyterian/Columbia University Medical Center in New York. His father, who is also retired, was a teacher at Roosevelt High School in Roosevelt, N.Y.
The couple met in 2012 through mutual friends at the Voices Annual Gala for Brotherhood/Sister Sol in New York.
