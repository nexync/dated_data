Nana Aba Wilberforce and Maj. Jack Axel Clark were married Nov. 4 at the Exchange Ballroom in Portland, Ore. Tabari Dossett, a Universal Life minister and friend of the couple, officiated.
Ms. Wilberforce, 31, is an antitrust lawyer in Washington for the law firm WilmerHale. She graduated from Stanford and received both a law degree and a master’s degree in public health from Columbia.
She is the daughter of Adjoa D. Wilberforce of Laguna Hills, Calif., and Sidney H. Wilberforce of Takoradi, Ghana. The bride’s mother is a registered nurse in Laguna Hills for the Saddleback Memorial Medical Center.
Major Clark, 36, is an Air Force commander of the 87 Contracting Squadron at Joint Base McGuire-Dix-Lakehurst. He graduated from the University of Portland and received a master’s degree in extension studies management from Harvard.
He is a son of Jamie Carlson Lauer of Klamath Falls, Ore., and Dennis W. Clark of Yreka, Calif. The groom’s mother is a school counselor at Bonanza Schools in Bonanza, Ore. His father retired as an agriculture teacher at Union High School in Union, Ore. The groom is also a stepson of Karen Clark and Mike S. Lauer.
The couple met on Tinder in July 2015.
