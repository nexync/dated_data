But condoning military operations that have displaced more than 620,000 Rohingya Muslims — and that have featured atrocities that amount to ethnic cleansing — will not nurture democratic change.
United States senators and human rights groups have called for targeted measures against senior Myanmar military commanders and officials responsible for atrocities: narrow economic and travel restrictions that could help press the military to end its abusive operations.
Such measures alone may not end the crisis, but along with other coordinated international action, will accomplish much more than sticking with a status quo that has had such a high human cost.
The writer is Asia advocacy director for Human Rights Watch.
