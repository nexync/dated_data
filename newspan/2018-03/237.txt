Lisa Margaret Cohen and Hershel Alan Kleinberg are to be married March 25 at Stone Tower Winery, a vineyard and winery in Leesburg, Va. Paul R. Michel, a retired chief judge of the United States Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit in Washington, officiated.
Ms. Cohen, 49, is a consultant based in Alexandria, Va., where she focuses on intercultural and diversity issues in organizations. Until November 2017, she was the chief of training at the United States Peace Corps in Washington. She is on the board of Cultural Vistas, a nonprofit group in New York and Washington facilitating overseas work experiences. She graduated from Cornell, and received a master’s degree in international education from Harvard.
She is the daughter of Doris M. Cohen of Audubon, Pa., and the late Dr. Richard S. Cohen. The bride’s father was a gastroenterologist and internist at the Mid Hudson Medical Group in Fishkill, N.Y., and Vassar Brothers Medical Center in Poughkeepsie, N.Y. Her mother is the president of the Jewish Interest Group at Shannondell at Valley Forge, a retirement community in Audubon.
Mr. Kleinberg, 53, is the executive vice president and the chief technology officer in the Alexandria office of Senior Market Sales, a family-run national insurance marketing company based in Omaha; the groom’s father, Milton M. Kleinberg, is the chief executive officer. The groom graduated cum laude from Washington University in St. Louis, and also received a master’s degree in computer engineering from the University of Southern California and a law degree from Georgetown.
He is the son of Marsha A. Kleinberg and Mr. Kleinberg of Omaha.
The couple met through JDate in January 2017, which turned out to be Mr. Kleinberg’s first and only online date, while she had dabbled.
