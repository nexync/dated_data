Bronwyn Conwell Roantree and Larry Bull were married Oct. 22 at the Orient Congregational Church, United Church of Christ, in Orient, N.Y. The Rev. Dr. Ann M. Van Cleef, the church’s pastor, performed the ceremony.
The bride, 36, who is keeping her name, is a second-year law student at Fordham. She graduated cum laude from Columbia and received a Ph.D. in religion and human rights from Harvard.
She is a daughter of Kathryn Vogan Roantree of Nashville and the late Thomas C. Roantree III.
The groom, 49, is an actor based in New York. Among his roles was Henry Bolingbroke in “Richard II” and “Henry IV,” Parts I and II, at the Utah Shakespeare Festival in Cedar City, Utah, from 2013 to 2015. He graduated from Boston University.
The groom’s previous marriage ended in divorce.
He is a son of Priscilla Terry Bull of Orient and the late Jasper D. Bull, who lived in Bangor, Me.
The couple were introduced in 2004 by mutual friends in a theater lobby when she went to see an Off Off Broadway play in which he was appearing, as were two of her college friends. After the show, she joined them and other cast members for drinks.
