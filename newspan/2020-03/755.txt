Experts offer advice on how parents can help adolescents get the facts straight and be prepared.
This article is part of the developing Coronavirus coverage, and may be outdated. Go here for the latest.
Here are tips from experts on how to help them.
The psychologist Lisa Damour, author of “Under Pressure: Confronting the Epidemic of Stress and Anxiety in Girls,” said parents can help lessen their anxiety, and that of their kids, by learning all they can about the new coronavirus and how to protect themselves.
If your emergency preparation plans include stocking up on groceries and toiletries, as you would before a big snowstorm or hurricane, you might invite your kids to go the store with you. Having a stash of their favorite snacks in the pantry could help them feel prepared.
With spring break on the horizon for many schoolchildren, Dr. Damour said that if families decide to cancel trips, it’s good to be upfront about it.
