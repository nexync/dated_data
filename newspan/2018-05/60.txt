Dr. Elizabeth Anne Clark and Dr. Nathan Woo Furukawa were married May 26 at the Pacific Tower, a caterer and events space in Seattle. Dr. Elizabeth B. Loft, a Universal Life minister and a friend of the couple, officiated.
The bride and groom met at the University of Washington, from which each received both a medical degree and a master’s degree in public health.
Dr. Clark, 34, is a fourth-year resident in obstetrics and gynecology at the University of New Mexico Hospital in Albuquerque. In July, she is to be begin a fellowship in reproductive health and will be an associate in gynecology and obstetrics at Emory University’s School of Medicine in Atlanta. She graduated from Rice University, and from 2006 to 2008, was a Peace Corps volunteer in Potrerillo, Peru, working on sanitation projects.
She is the daughter of the Rev. Martha C. Brimm and Dr. Richard V. Clark of Durham, N.C. The bride’s father, an endocrinologist, retired as a clinical trials research scientist in Durham for GlaxoSmithKline, the British pharmaceutical company. Her mother, an Episcopal priest, retired as a chaplain at the Franklin Correctional Center in Bunn, N.C.
Dr. Furukawa, 31, is an internist on staff at Presbyterian Hospital in Albuquerque. In July, he is to become an epidemic intelligence service officer, working on H.I.V. prevention, at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention in Atlanta. He graduated from Seattle University. He is also a senior fellow at Humanity in Action, a human rights organization in New York.
He is a son of Mae W. Woo of Billings, Mont., and Dean K. Furukawa of Pablo, Mont. The groom’s father, a social worker, teaches chemical dependency counseling at Salish Kootenai College in Pablo.
The bride and groom met in medical school, but it wasn’t until after their second year, when he was conducting tuberculosis research in Lima, Peru, and she was visiting Peace Corps friends, that they became friendly, and soon fell in love.
