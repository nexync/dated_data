Laurel Trigg Rapp, a daughter of Mary K. Trigg and Ronald J. Rapp of Bernardsville, N.J., was married Aug. 27 to Matthew Justin Davis, a son of Barbara Valente Davis and Michael W. Davis of Evanston, Ill. Thomas M. Frayne, a friend of the groom’s family who became a minister of the American Marriage Ministries, officiated at Mount Hope Farm in Bristol, R.I.
Ms. Rapp, 32, is keeping her name. She is the special assistant to the assistant secretary for international-organization affairs at the State Department, working on American involvement in the United Nations and other multilateral institutions. She graduated from Brown and received a master’s in public policy from Harvard.
Mr. Davis, 39, is the author of “When Things Get Dark: A Mongolian Winter’s Tale” and is the founding director of the Alan Cheuse International Writers Center at George Mason University in Fairfax, Va. He graduated from the University of Missouri, received a master’s in nonfiction writing from the University of Iowa and has another master’s in international relations from Johns Hopkins.
The couple met in February 2012 at a mutual friend’s cocktail party in Washington.
