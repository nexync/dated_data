An article on Page 8 about the premiere of Missy Mazzoli’s opera “Proving Up” misstated the company that produced it. It was Washington National Opera, in January, not Opera Omaha, which gave the second performances in April.
Because of an editing error, an article last Sunday about Simone Leigh erroneously attributed a distinction to her sculpture “Brick House,” for the High Line in Manhattan. It is not her first public project; she previously displayed sculptures in Marcus Garvey Park in Manhattan from 2016 to 2017.
Because of a technical error, the combined print and e-book nonfiction best-seller list in this weekend’s Book Review duplicates the print hardcover nonfiction list. The correct rankings for the combined list can be found at nytimes.com/best-sellers.
A review on Aug. 5 about “Metamorphica,” by Zachary Mason, misidentified the author’s last book. It was “Void Star,” published in 2017; it was not “The Lost Books of the Odyssey” (2010).
An article on Aug. 26 about presidential judicial appointments failed to attribute a characterization of the political leanings of several United States Courts of Appeals. It is President Trump’s supporters in the conservative legal movement who believe that the Sixth, Seventh and Eighth Circuit Courts were “liberal” before recent appointments.
An article on Sept. 2 about the actor Riz Ahmed misspelled the given name of a British musician. She is Susheela Raman, not Shusheela.
Because of a production error, an article on Sept. 2 about flooding in Houston during Hurricane Harvey referred incorrectly in a highlighted quotation to the time of Wayne Dailey’s fourth call to 911. As the article correctly stated, it took place at 10:15 a.m., not 10:15 p.m.
