Colleen Marie McCarriagher and Kevin Raymond Siskar were married Aug. 18 at the Bourne Mansion in Oakdale, N.Y. The Rev. Dr. Janine H. Burns, an interfaith minister, officiated.
Mrs. Siskar, 29, is an art therapist and a visual arts teacher at Public School 172, the Beacon School of Excellence, in Brooklyn, and also supervises students in New York University’s graduate art therapy program. She graduated from the State University of New York College at Buffalo, and received a master’s degree in art therapy from N.Y.U.
She is the daughter of Denise L. McCarriagher and Patrick S. McCarriagher of Clarence, N.Y. The bride’s father is the warranty claims administrator at Paddock Chevrolet, a car dealership in Kenmore, N.Y.
Mr. Siskar, 28, is the managing director in Manhattan at the Founder Institute, a company that helps start-ups get going, and is also an associate partner in Outbound Ventures, which invests in new companies, and is a founder of the NYC Innovation Collective, an organization in Manhattan that brings accelerators and incubators and other entities that support start-ups together to support their mutual interests. He graduated from SUNY Buffalo.
He is a son of Patricia A. Siskar of East Amherst, N.Y., and the late Raymond A. Siskar. The groom’s mother is the food service director at St. Mary School Swormville, a Roman Catholic elementary and middle school in Swormville, N.Y., from which the groom graduated. His father was the intelligence transportation systems manager in Buffalo for the New York State Thruway Authority.
The couple met in 2009 when both worked waiting tables at the Country Club of Buffalo, which is in Williamsville, N.Y.
