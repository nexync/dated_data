The Encounters column last Sunday, about the actor Zachary Levi during a visit to Midtown Comics, misidentified the man who asked Mr. Levi to sign back issues of “Shazam!” It was Rich Capobianco, the store’s sales manager, not Henry Varona.
A picture caption with an article on Page 8 about suggestions for new characters in a Spider-Man sequel misstates the first name of one of the Spideys. He is Miles — not Milo — Morales.
A review on Dec. 2 about “Mad Scenes and Exit Arias: The Death of the New York City Opera and the Future of Opera in America,” by Heidi Waleson, misidentified City Opera’s inaugural production after the company’s move to the New York State Theater at Lincoln Center in 1966. It was Alberto Ginastera’s “Don Rodrigo,” which opened on Feb. 22 that year; it was not Handel’s “Giulio Cesare,” which opened on Sept. 27.
A picture caption on Dec. 2 with a review of Christine Otten’s novel “The Last Poets,” using information from Getty Images, misidentified a member of the hip-hop forerunners known as the Last Poets. He was Nilaja Obabi, not Abiodun Oyewole.
An article on Dec. 16 about the war in Yemen misstated one aspect of the involvement of the United States in the war in Yemen. In 2015, the United States Navy began assigning warships to the region to prevent weapons shipments from reaching Yemen. The United States did not send warships to help Saudi Arabia enforce a blockade.
An article last Sunday about training feral cats to kill mice for homeowners misstated the full name of the A.S.P.C.A. It is the American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals, not the Animal Society for Prevention of Cruelty to Animals.
