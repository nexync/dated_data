The divisions are deep, but Britain’s ruling party knows how to stand together when the time comes.
Mr. Bale is a professor of politics at Queen Mary, University of London.
LONDON — So Prime Minister Theresa May lives to fight another day. She won Wednesday’s vote of no confidence: 200 members of her party stood by her; 117 did not. Indeed, under the Conservative Party’s rules, it will be another year before her opponents in the party can try to unseat her as leader.
Tim Bale (@proftimbale) is a professor of politics at Queen Mary University of London and the author of “The Conservative Party: From Thatcher to Cameron,” among other books.
