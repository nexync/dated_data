Scientists in the U.S. and Mexico have mapped the DNA of several varieties, work that could help the fruit survive the effects of climate change.
The history of the avocado dates back to Mesoamerica, where the fruit was known by the Aztec word “āhuacatl,” sometimes used as slang for “testicle.” Thousands of years later, Mr. Granger is often credited with being the first chef to serve avocado toast, which is now a staple of the millennial diet, a symbol of modern decadence and an Instagram sensation.
