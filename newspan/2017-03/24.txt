Zoia Alexanian and Adam Richard Pearce were married Feb. 28. Alicia Fuentes, a staff member in the office of the New York City Clerk, officiated at the Manhattan Marriage Bureau.
Ms. Alexanian, 30, is keeping her name. She is a consultant at Tager & Company, a strategy and communications firm in New York. She graduated magna cum laude from Williams College, and received a master’s degree in English studies from Cambridge University.
She is a daughter of Victoria Alexanian and Sergei Alexanian of Ewing, N.J. The bride’s father is a supervisor at Impax Laboratories, a specialty pharmaceutical company in Bridgewater, N.J. Her mother is a medical assistant at a doctor’s office in Pennington, N.J.
Mr. Pearce, 27, is a graphics editor at The New York Times. He graduated from the University of Texas at Dallas.
He is a son of Sharon Rhebergen Pearce and Larry J. Pearce of Oak Park, Ill. The groom’s mother is a librarian at Longfellow Elementary School in Oak Park. His father is a band director at Morton West High School in Berwyn, Ill.
The couple met through OkCupid in 2013.
