Colonial-era homes line the streets of The Point in Newport, R.I. Climate change is forcing experts to reimagine the future of historic preservation here.
Flood vents on cellar walls open automatically. Water is allowed to flow through, reducing the risk of water pressure damaging the foundation. The basement has been filled above the water table, and utilities have been relocated to higher floors.
Basements can be turned into cisterns to collect floodwater and slowly release it into the stormwater system once the flood has passed. Entire neighborhoods can be equipped with larger capacity storm water pipes, or houses can be made to float on the rising floodwater.
