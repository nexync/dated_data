Corrections that appeared in print on Wednesday, July 29, 2020.
An article on Tuesday about Confederate identity in Lexington, Va., misspelled the given name of a member of the Lexington City Council. She is Marylin Alexander, not Marilyn.
An article on Saturday about Jacksonville’s brief time as the future home of the Republican National Convention described incorrectly the size of Jacksonville, Fla. It is the largest city by land area in the contiguous United States, not the continental United States.
Because of an editing error, an article on Tuesday about a postponed game between the Yankees and the Philadelphia Phillies described incorrectly the Major League Baseball summer camp. Every team had workouts at their home stadiums, not all teams except Toronto.
An article on Tuesday about the 1980 Olympic boycott described incorrectly the circumstances in which Vice President Walter Mondale apologized for the boycott. He made the statement during an interview with The Wall Street Journal, not during a town hall event with athletes.
A brief report in the Front Burner column last Wednesday about Smorgasburg’s food-ordering service, Smorg to Go, using information from a publicist, misstated the type of meat served by #Gogi, one of the vendors. It is beef barbecue, not pork.
