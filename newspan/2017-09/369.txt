Remy Blayne Sanders and Craig Benjamin Dowd were married Sept. 9 in Tarrytown, N.Y. Jordan Sanders, the brother of the bride who became a Universal Life minister for the occasion, officiated at Blue Hill at Stone Barns.
Mrs. Dowd, 29, is a psychotherapist working in private practice for JMW Therapy in New York. She graduated cum laude from the University of Pennsylvania and received a master’s degree in social work at Columbia.
She is a daughter of Pamela S. Sanders and Arthur D. Sanders of Greenwich, Conn. The bride’s father is the founder and president of Sanders Equities, a real estate investment and management firm in Greenwich. His mother is on the board of White Columns, a nonprofit art organization in New York that offers support and exhibition space to contemporary artists.
Mr. Dowd, also 29, is a vice president in the real estate institutional markets group at MUFG Union Bank in New York. He graduated from Georgetown.
He is a son of Patricia A. Dowd and Benjamin M. Dowd of East Northport, N.Y. The groom’s mother is a social studies and law teacher at Lindenhurst High School in Lindenhurst, N.Y. His father is a 7-Eleven franchisee in Huntington Station, N.Y.
The couple met in January 2015 at Emerald Pub, a bar in New York.
