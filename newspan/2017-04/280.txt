An article on Friday about a massive bomb dropped on an Islamic State cave complex in Afghanistan misstated the cost of the weapon used in the attack. The bomb costs an estimated $170,000 per weapon, according to the Air Force, not $16 million.
An article on April 11 about fans at the Yankees’ home opener misidentified the state in which the town of Pearl River is located. It is New York, not New Jersey.
An article on April 10 about Sergio García’s victory at the Masters referred incompletely to the college golf career of his fiancée, Angela Akins. She played first at Texas Christian and then at Texas, not just at Texas Christian.
A theater review on Tuesday about “It Will All Work Out,” at Dixon Place, misstated the given name of the director of the show. She is Eliza Laytner, not Elise.
Because of an editing error, a report in the “Arts, Briefly” column on Monday misidentified the actor appearing in a groundhog costume in a photograph of the Broadway musical “Groundhog Day.” Raymond J. Lee, not Barrett Doss, wore the costume.
A review last Wednesday about Torishin, a Japanese restaurant in Hell’s Kitchen, misstated the name of a spice blend provided on tables and counters. It is shichimi, not shichi.
