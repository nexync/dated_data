NATURE: HOTEL ARMADILLO 8 p.m. on PBS. Arnaud Desbiez, a Brazilian conservation biologist, and his team in the Pantanal, the world’s largest tropical wetland, explain why the secretive giant armadillo — which spends about 75 percent of its life burrowed underground — is vital to the region’s biodiversity.
NOVA: HOLOCAUST ESCAPE TUNNEL 9 p.m. on PBS (check local listings). Last June, archaeologists announced that they had uncovered a forgotten tunnel: a 100-foot-long passageway, five or more feet below the surface, that 80 Jews dug mostly by hand as they tried to escape from a Nazi extermination site in Lithuania during World War II. Here, children of 11 of those men, who evaded what were known as the Ponar killing pits — where as many as 100,000 people were shot at close range, with their bodies dumped or burned — recount their fathers’ lives.
