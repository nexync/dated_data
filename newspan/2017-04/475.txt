Eleanor Cole Boyle, a daughter of Cynthia Maresca Boyle and John N. Boyle III of Campobello, S.C., was married April 1 to Richard Ragsdale Reutter, a son of Caroline Ragsdale Reutter and Chick Reutter of Spartanburg, S.C. The Rev. Michael E. Bowers, a United Methodist minister, officiated at Jacksons Grove United Methodist Church in Landrum, S.C., where he is the pastor.
The bride, 33, who goes by Cole, graduated from Washington and Lee University. She works in Spartanburg as the director of sales for the Southeast region at Dstillery, an advertising technology firm in New York.
The bride’s parents own Freehold Financial Group, a wealth advisory firm in Landrum.
The groom, 35, graduated from Hobart and William Smith Colleges in Geneva, N.Y., and received an M.B.A. from Wake Forest. He is the vice president overseeing operations at Caroline’s Cakes, a mail-order business in Spartanburg; his mother is the president and founder.
The groom’s father is a sales executive and a designer of satellite networks at AVL Technologies, a satellite communications firm in Asheville, N.C.
The couple met in 2011 at the rehearsal party before her sister’s wedding, in which she was the maid of honor and he was a groomsman.
