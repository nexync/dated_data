The wine glasses clinked and the banter was jovial as dozens of A-listers in evening wear — including Ryan Reynolds, Sarah Paulson and Trevor Noah — settled in over plates of poached Maine lobster Tuesday night in the vaulting atrium of Jazz at Lincoln Center overlooking Columbus Circle.
Everything, in other words, was in place for the lavish Time 100 Gala celebrating 100 “activists, artists, leaders and entrepreneurs who are changing the world,” as the evening’s program put it.
This certainly never happens at the Met Gala. Then again, the annual Time 100 bash is a most unusual gala, a glittering celebration not of celebrity, but of influence.
Sprinkled among the standard-issue entertainment luminaries (Mia Farrow, Samantha Bee and Barry Jenkins, the director of “Moonlight”) were the likes of Thelma Aldana, the attorney general of Guatemala, whose anticorruption campaign helped topple the government, and Raed Saleh, the head of Syrian Civil Defense, the volunteer rescue force known as the White Helmets.
Alas, to have a seat at that table.
In fact, those world leaders were predictable no-shows (although Mr. Trump did attend last year).
No wonder Gavin Grimm was looking a little star-struck. The transgender 17-year-old from Gloucester, Va., whose decision to use the boys’ bathroom at school ended up as a case before the United States Supreme Court, was standing alongside his mother, Deirdre Grimm. Wearing a baggy gray suit, he looked as if he had won a golden ticket from some highly politicized Willy Wonka as he surveyed the celebrity-packed room.
