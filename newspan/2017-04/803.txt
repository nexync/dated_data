For a longish stretch, fans of the Pulitzer-winning playwright Paula Vogel had cause for selfish worry. Season after season in the American theater brought plays by the extraordinary talents she nurtured as a teacher, first at Brown and later at Yale — Lynn Nottage, Sarah Ruhl, Martyna Majok, and the list goes on. But Ms. Vogel’s new work was scarce, seemingly back-burnered by her devotion to younger generations.
