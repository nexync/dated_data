Danielle Ilycia Cohen and Patrick Russell Forquer were married April 22 in an interfaith ceremony at the John and Mable Ringling Museum of Art in Sarasota, Fla. Rabbi Lewis Solomon officiated, with the Rev. Stephen Goyer, a Presbyterian minister, participating.
The bride, 30, is a vice president at HSBC Securities, the London bank, in the rate sales group in the New York office. She graduated magna cum laude from Colgate. She is a daughter of Marie Cohen and Roy Cohen of Summit, N.J. The bride’s father is a senior principal at the law firm Porzio, Bromberg & Newman in Morristown, N.J. Her mother is a managing partner at Marketing Insights, a strategic planning and branding consulting firm in Summit.
The groom, 31, is a senior account executive in New York at Appboy, which tracks growth and engagement for apps. He graduated from Duke. He is the son of Dr. Barbara N. Croft and David R. Forquer of Atlanta. The groom’s mother is a gynecologist at Piedmont Hospital there. His father is a professor and the assistant dean for executive programs at Georgia State University, also in Atlanta.
The couple met in Montauk, N.Y., in 2013.
