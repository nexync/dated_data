Sloane Camille Smith, the daughter of Dr. Camille Baptiste-Smith and Dr. Clarence E. Smith of Miami Beach, was married May 20 to Jemel Lee Saunders, the son of Brenda J. Gomillion of Baldwin, N.Y., and Robbie L. Saunders of Henrico, Va. The Very Rev. Douglas McCaleb, an Episcopal priest, performed the ceremony at Trinity Episcopal Cathedral in Miami.
Mrs. Saunders, 29, is a clinical researcher at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center in New York. She graduated from Hofstra and received a master’s degree from SUNY Downstate Medical Center.
The bride’s mother is an attending physician in the department of emergency medicine at University of Miami/Jackson Memorial Medical Center in Miami. Her father, who also worked in Miami, retired as the medical director for both the division of managed care of the Jackson Health System and the Jackson Memorial Hospital Health Plan of the Public Health Trust of Miami-Dade County, Fla.
Mr. Saunders, 28, works in the finance department for HLW International, an architecture and design firm in New York. He graduated from Seton Hall. His mother retired as a New York City correction officer.
The couple were introduced in 2010 by a mutual friend in Rockville Centre, N.Y., and began dating in 2013.
