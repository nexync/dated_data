Saudi Women Can Drive Now. Will That Hurt Saudi Women?
Ms. Aldosari researches Saudi gender dynamics at Harvard.
Concessions on women’s rights in Saudi Arabia often appear during times of turmoil or transition.
Women are at the core not only of the Prince Mohammed charm offensive but of his larger vision to diversify the economy and to pare its huge welfare state. The attractions of employing more women in an economy trying to shift away from an overdependence on oil are obvious: The private sector is underdeveloped, centered on construction and retail, and largely dependent on state funding; at the same time, women’s work force participation hovers at 21 percent, even though Saudi women are among the most educated in the region.
