Alison Faith Hines and Nathan Vozza Koch were married Feb. 18 at the Garrison, an event space in Garrison, N.Y. Benjamin Malbin, a minister with American Marriage Ministries and a friend of the couple, officiated.
Mrs. Koch, 31, is a digital-exhibitions project manager at the National September 11 Memorial and Museum in New York. She graduated magna cum laude from New York University.
She is the daughter of Katherine B. Hines and William C. Hines of Mahopac, N.Y. The bride’s father, a retired New York City Fire Department battalion chief, is now a New York-based consultant on issues regarding emergency-response preparedness. Her father’s experiences at the World Trade Center site, she said, were “a huge reason I work where I do. And why it is so unbelievably rewarding.” Her mother is a special-education teacher in the Beacon City School District in Beacon, N.Y.
Mr. Koch, 31, owns Enveekay, a theater production and management company in New York. He is currently the executive producer of the Off Broadway production “Sweeney Todd” at the Barrow Street Theater in New York. He graduated with honors from N.Y.U.
He is the son of Nancy Vozza Koch and Peter M. Koch of East Setauket, N.Y. The groom’s mother is retired from the Smithtown Central School District in Smithtown, N.Y., where she was a middle-school guidance counselor. His father is a professor of physics at Stony Brook University in New York, and is currently on sabbatical at the City College of New York.
The couple were introduced by a mutual friend in New York in January 2014.
