Lyric Ingrid Chen and Haryle Anthony Kaldis were married June 10. Dr. Leana S. Wen, a friend of the bride who was ordained for this ceremony by the American Marriage Ministries, officiated at the Barn at Glistening Pond, an events venue in Falls, Pa.
The bride, 32, is a law clerk for Judge Legrome D. Davis of the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania, in Philadelphia. She graduated from the University of Michigan, received a master’s degree in politics from Oxford, where she studied as a Marshall Scholar, and received her law degree from Harvard. She is a daughter of Rita J. Chou of Columbia, S.C. Her mother is an associate professor of social work at the University of South Carolina there.
The groom, 35, is an associate at Cozen O’Connor, a law firm in Philadelphia. He graduated from George Washington University and received his law degree from Columbia. From 2001 to 2004, he served as a paratrooper in the Army and was stationed at Fort Bragg, N.C., in the 82nd Airborne Division. He is the son of Mercedes N. Kaldis and Theodoros H. Kaldis of West Nyack, N.Y. His father retired as an owner of the Hillside Diner, which was in Oakland, N.J.
The couple met in 2012, on their first day of work as associates in the Washington law office of Wilmer Cutler Pickering Hale & Dorr.
