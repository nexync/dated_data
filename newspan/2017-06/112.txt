Chloe Bradley Wardropper and Vanessa Juliet Lauber were married June 24. William Voinot-Baron, a friend of the couple who became a Universal Life minister, officiated at the Hilltop, an events venue in Spring Green, Wis.
Ms. Wardropper, 31, is a postdoctoral fellow in the forestry and natural resources department at Purdue University in West Lafayette, Ind., where she conducts research on conservation practices. She graduated from Wesleyan University and received a doctoral degree in environmental studies from the University of Wisconsin-Madison. She is to become an assistant professor of integrated water resources at the University of Idaho in Moscow in November.
She is the daughter of Laurel E. Bradley of Northfield, Minn., and Ian B. Wardropper of New York. Ms. Wardropper’s mother is the director and curator of the Perlman Teaching Museum at Carleton College in Northfield. Her father is the director of the Frick Collection, the New York museum. Ms. Wardropper is also the stepdaughter of Sarah A. McNear and of Samuel G. Demas.
Ms. Lauber, 30, is a doctoral candidate in English literature at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, and is to begin studying for a law degree at Harvard in August. She graduated summa cum laude from Wofford College in Spartanburg, S.C.
She is the daughter of Melanie A. Lauber and K. David Lauber of Greer, S.C. Ms. Lauber’s father is a marketing manager at Span-America Medical Systems, a manufacturer in Greenville, S.C.. Her mother retired as a kindergarten teacher at Columbus Elementary School, in Rome, N.Y.
The couple met in the fall of 2012 through a mutual friend.
