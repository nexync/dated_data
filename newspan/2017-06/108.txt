Dr. Nayoung Lee and Dr. Richard Ro were married June 10 in New York. The Rev. Thomas J. Holahan performed the ceremony at the Roman Catholic Church of St. Paul the Apostle.
Dr. Lee, 29, will complete the second year of a dermatology residency at Jackson Memorial Hospital of the University of Miami this month. She graduated summa cum laude from Dartmouth and received her medical degree from Columbia.
She is the daughter of Sonhwa Na and Song Lee of Boxborough, Mass. Her father is an independent accountant in Boxborough. Her mother is the director for research and development in the Waltham, Mass., office of Dassault Systèmes, a French software developer.
Dr. Ro, 32, is an assistant professor of clinical medicine at the Columbia College of Physicians and Surgeons in New York, and a cardiologist at Mount Sinai Medical Center in Miami. He graduated cum laude from the City College of New York and received a medical degree from Stony Brook University.
He is a son of Susan Jung Ro and Dong Sup Ro of Auburndale, Queens. The groom’s father is a letter carrier in White Plains for the United States Postal Service.
The couple met in 2014 when both were at Mount Sinai St. Luke’s Hospital in New York, she as a medical intern and he as a cardiology fellow.
