An article on June 6 about moves by neighboring Arab nations to isolate Qatar erroneously included one type of institution in describing the construction boom in the country. Though Qatar has created branches of major international universities, it has not done so for international museums.
Because of an editing error, an article on Tuesday misstated the year that Bob Lang bought the land on which Erin Hills, the site of the 2017 U.S. Open, was built. It was 2001, not 2011.
An article on June 7 about efforts by colleges to help students deal with depression, anxiety or suicidal ideas misstated the name of an organization at U.C.L.A. that will monitor 100,000 people to identify genetic and environmental causes of depression. It is the U.C.L.A. Depression Grand Challenge, not the Depressions Grand Challenge.
