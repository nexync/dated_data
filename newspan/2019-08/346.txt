BEVERLY HILLS, Calif. — Ryan Murphy’s popular television series “American Crime Story” will return for a third season that will tackle the Bill Clinton-Monica Lewinsky scandal, FX announced on Tuesday afternoon.
Ryan Murphy with the Golden Globe for “The Assassination of Gianni Versace: American Crime Story,” the second season of his series.
Expectations will be huge. The first installment of the FX series, “The People v. O.J. Simpson” set ratings records for FX in 2016 and won the Emmy and the Golden Globe for best limited series. The second installment, “The Assassination of Gianni Versace,” which came out last year, also won the best limited series Emmy and Globe.
The playwright Sarah Burgess will write the new season, which FX said would begin on Sept. 27, 2020. It will go into production in February.
