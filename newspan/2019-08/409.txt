Dr. Julia Collins Kearney and Dr. Krishna Amuluru were married Aug. 17 at Morgan Manufacturing, an events space in Chicago. Anu Malhotra, a Hindu priestess, officiated.
The bride, 30, is a resident physician in the department of obstetrics and gynecology at New York University/Bellevue Hospital. She graduated from Princeton and received a medical degree from Columbia.
She is a daughter of Claire Edwards Kearney and Theron Vincent Kearney III of New York. The bride’s father is a senior ship broker for Quincannon Associates in New York, where he brokers deals to sell oil tankers, freighters and other ships or their cargo. Her mother, who is retired, was a first mate aboard a Chevron oil tanker working on a route between San Francisco to Alaska.
The groom, 38, is a neurointerventional radiologist at Goodman Campbell Brain and Spine, a neurosurgery group of physicians based in Indianapolis. He graduated from Columbia and received a medical degree from Northwestern.
He is the son of Jaladurga Amuluru and Prabhakar R. Amuluru of Livingston, N.J. The groom’s mother retired as an attending internal medicine physician at the Hackensack Veterans Hospital Ambulatory Care Center in Hackensack, N.J. His father retired as a cardiac anesthesiologist at St. Michael’s Medical Center Anesthesia in Newark.
The couple met in September 2013 at a mutual friend’s birthday party in New York.
