Have you ever been mistaken for being pregnant? Or have you wrongly assumed someone else was pregnant?
We asked readers to share their stories following an article by Elizabeth Yuko, “When You Look Pregnant, but You’re Not.” Over a thousand people wrote in about their mostly mortifying experiences. Some offered insights about the health conditions that can cause a woman to look pregnant. And some women just decided to go with it, accepting the well-intentioned kindness of strangers toward their imaginary fetus. Below is a selection of responses that have been edited and condensed.
